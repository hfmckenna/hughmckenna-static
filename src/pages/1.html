INDUSTRIAL
SOURDOUGH

A high-hydration recipe engineered for home ovens. No kneading required, just time and temperature control. Results in a blistered crust and open crumb structure.

PREP: 24 HRS
COOK: 45 MIN
YIELD: 2 LOAVES
INPUTS

INGREDIENTS

  • 800g Strong White Bread Flour
  • 200g Whole Wheat Flour
  • 750g Water (Room Temp)
  • 200g Active Sourdough Starter
  • 20g Fine Sea Salt

EXECUTION

01

Autolyse

Mix the flour and water in a large bowl until no dry flour remains. Cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 1 hour. This allows the gluten structure to begin forming before the fermentation begins.

02

Inoculation & Salt

Add the sourdough starter and salt. Pinch the dough with your fingers to incorporate the new ingredients. Perform a "Rubaud" mix for 5 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and extensible.

03

Bulk Fermentation

Transfer to a clean container. Perform 4 sets of "Stretch and Folds" spaced 30 minutes apart. After the last fold, let the dough rest until it has increased in volume by 50% (approx 3-4 hours depending on temperature).

Note: Look for bubbles on the surface and a jiggly texture.
04

Bake

Preheat dutch oven to 250°C. Score the dough. Bake covered for 20 minutes. Remove lid, lower temp to 230°C and bake for another 20-25 minutes until dark golden brown.
